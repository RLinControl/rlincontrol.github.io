Course Overview

  • University: Iran University of Science and Technology (IUST)
  • School / Department: School of Electrical Engineering – Control Group
  • Level: Graduate (Master’s and PhD)
  • Offered Semester: Fall 2025

This course is part of the graduate curriculum in the Control Group of the School of Electrical Engineering at Iran University of Science and Technology. It is intended for Master’s and PhD students with a background in control systems, machine learning, or related fields. The course combines theoretical foundations with hands-on practical applications.

Course Objectives

  • A solid understanding of core RL concepts: bandit problems, exploration vs. exploitation, and Markov Decision Processes (MDPs)
  • Familiarity with classical solution methods: dynamic programming, Monte Carlo, and temporal-difference learning (e.g., Q-Learning, SARSA)
  • An introduction to neural networks and deep reinforcement learning
  • Exposure to advanced methods such as Actor-Critic and DDPG
  • Application of RL techniques in optimal control: Bellman equation, policy/value iteration, LQR, LQT, and H∞ control
  • Hands-on experience through coding assignments using Python (PyTorch, Gymnasium, Stable-Baselines3) and MATLAB
  • Engagement with current trends and simulation practices through seminar sessions

Prerequisites

  • Probability and Statistics
  • Modern Control Theory (including optimal control concepts)
  • Basic programming skills in Python and MATLAB

Familiarity with machine learning concepts is helpful but not required.

Tools & Resources

  • Programming Languages & Libraries:
    • Python and MATLAB (both are used throughout the course)
    • PyTorch for implementing deep learning models
    • Gymnasium and Stable-Baselines3 for RL environments and algorithm baselines

Main Textbooks

  • Reinforcement Learning: An Introduction by Richard S. Sutton and Andrew G. Barto
  • Optimal Control by Frank L. Lewis, Draguna Vrabie, and Vassilis L. Syrmos (for the control-focused part of the course)

Recommended Supplementary Courses

  • David Silver’s Reinforcement Learning Course (DeepMind)
  • CS285: Deep Reinforcement Learning at UC Berkeley
  • Hugging Face Deep RL Course (Free)
